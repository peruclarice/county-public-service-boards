he County Public Service Boards National Consultative Forum (CPSBs-NCF) is a duly registered society as a national association of all the forty-seven (47) County Public Service Boards (CPSBs) in the Republic of Kenya.to cooperate and collaborate on matters of mutual interest in line with their respective constitutional and legal manadates The Constitution of Kenya (CoK) 2010 sets out in Article 235 the establishment of a County Public Service Board in each of the 47 Counties in Kenya with the mandate to staff County Governments within a framework of uniform norms and standards prescribed by an Act of Parliament, for (a) the establishing and abolishing offices in its public service; (b) appointing persons to hold or act in those offices, and confirming appointments; and (c) exercising disciplinary control over and removing persons holding or acting in those offices, which was duly operationalized by the enactment of the County Governments Act ( CGA) 2012, which was amended in 2020 to emphasize its operational independence.

The Functions of the CPSBs are outlined in section 59 of the CGA, 2012, which includes, among others, the functions and powers listed in the excerpt of Section 59 below:
“59. Functions and powers of a County Public Service Board (1) The functions of the County Public Service Board shall be, on behalf of the county government, to— (a) establish and abolish offices in the county public service; (b) appoint persons to hold or act in offices of the county public service including in the Boards of cities and urban areas within the county and to confirm appointments; (c) exercise disciplinary control over, and remove, persons holding or acting in those offices as provided for under this Part; (d) prepare regular reports for submission to the county assembly on the execution of the functions of the Board; (e) promote in the county public service the values and principles referred to in Articles 10 and 232; (f) evaluate and report to the county assembly on the extent to which the values and principles referred to in Articles 10 and 232 are complied with in the county public service; (g) facilitate the development of coherent, integrated human resource planning and budgeting for personnel emoluments in counties; (h) advise the county government on human resource management and development; (i) advise county government on implementation and monitoring of the national performance management system in counties; (j) make recommendations to the Salaries and Remuneration Commission, on behalf of the county government, on the remuneration, pensions and gratuities for county public service employees.”

by Hetmayer

The CPSBs-NCF has been in existence since 2014 and has been convening an Annual Convention once every year to bring together all its members to network and discuss contemporary issues affecting their mandates.

LEADERSHIP & PROFILE OF THE COUNTY PUBLIC SERVICE BOARDS NATIONAL CONSULTATIVE FORUM

The National Forum has transitioned through two past leadership cycles and the current office is now under the leadership of Hon. Jared Kopiyo, National Chairperson of the CPSB National Consultative Forum. The CPSB National Consultative Forum is led by a 9-member Executive Committee which comprises the 9 Regional Representatives elected by Members in a General Meeting for a term of 2 years as per the Forum Constitution which provides for 4 Office Bearers, namely national Chairperson, National Vice Chairperson, National Secretary-General and National Treasury and 5 other members, all of whom are regional representatives of the nine (9) regions listed in the table below.

During the 3rd Administration Cycle (2021-2024) of the Forum, the following members of the Executive Committee (Excom), whose profiles are as shown in the table below, were elected into office in April 2022 for a 2-year term.

The Excom runs the affairs of the National Forum with advice from Committees. Its seven (7) Committees and ToRs are shared as an attachment to this letter for your information and reference. Excom is also assisted in the delivery of its mandate by the 47-Member CPSBs National Steering Committee, which comprises a representative Nominated by each CPSB ahead of a General Meeting during an election year for a 2-year term for the Purpose of articulating each Board’s position, especially on matters of policy to guide the Forum. The Table below lists all the 47 CPSBs and their respective regions, whose chairperson, each nominates a Steering Committee Member of the Forum in writing within 3 months to the General election of each election year. Its main objective is to streamline efficiency and productivity in the County Public Service through sector reforms and the execution of functions according to the Constitution and legal mandate.

Excom runs the daily affairs of the Forum through 7 Committees, each led by an Excom Member, and draws membership from the Steering Committee Members and co-opted individual members of various CPSBs

  • County Public Service Delivery and Innovation Committee
  • Norms and Standards for the Management of Human Resources in the County Public Service Committee,
  • Legal Affairs and Welfare Committee,
  • Competencies for the County Public Service Committee,
  • County Public Service Transformation Committee,
  • Human Resource Planning and Succession Management Committee
  • Audit Committee,

The National Forum also makes policy decisions through a 47-member Steering Committee comprising one (1) member each from the 47 County Public Service Boards

All communications to the respective forty-seven (47) County Public Service Boards should be addressed to them through the Chairperson and/or Secretary-General of the National Forum.
